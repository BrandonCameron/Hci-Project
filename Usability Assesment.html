Usability Assement

Flexibility :

Youtube features variuos shortcuts that can be used by advanved users.
Users are able to learn of these commands naturally as certain shortcuts will be shown when hovering over the feature the shortcut is for.
This allows users who are unaware of these shortcuts to find out about them without being told by someoine else of finding it online.

However despite showing some shortcuts, theier are still shortcuts, such as frame
stepping, that a user cant find out about without searching for it.w

Aesthetic and minimalist design :

Youtube is great for only displaying required information. As seen in the pic above,
even tho multiple videos are displayed at once, we are only given the info we need on them,
that being views, creator, title and how long ago the video was uploaded to the website.

There are two pages i could find that don't follow the design of the rest
of the website and have less appeal as well as wasted space. These pages are the terms
page and the contact us page.

Recognition rather than recall :

Youtube makes sure its users are well informed of what different icons mean even if they look similar.
A good example in the pic is the two icons for 'history' and 'watch later'.
Despite these icons being simlar in design, because they are labeled users can identify them without thinking

The symbols on a videos timeline are for the most part self explanitory.
However two symbols, miniplayer and theature player are hard to identify
these symbols require the user to hover over them to understand what they do
and even then without using the feature users wont know what they do.

Error prevention :

Youtube is very clear when an error occurs.
The pic aboce illustrates how youtube informs the users why an error has occured in very simple language.

Despite youtube usually explaining their errors clearly youtube still falls victim to
errors without explaination as seen above.

Match between system and the real world :

Youtube uses very simple language throughout the website so that the users full understand
what is being communicated. This is important for the user to understand without needing external resources.
In the pic presented, found in the how does youtube work section, we see that they use simple language to
describe youtubes commitments to the commmunity.

Despite Youtube featuring simple language throught out the website, even
for complicated topics such as copyright, one area of the website where this
falters is the terms of service. The terms of service use more comlicated language
even though it is expected to be read by everyone making a youtube account.

Consistency :

Youtube has a consistency with other apps that are owned by google. The best examples is for most
google apps, The home button for the website is featured in the top left while the users profile is
located in the top right. Consistency is important as it allows users to understand new websites easier
based on past experience.

A bad example of cinsistency on yotube is in the how youtbe works section.
This part of youtbe features the home button at the top left however
despite looking the same as the normal home button, this button takes you
to the first page of how youtube works. This is in contrast to youtube studio
which features a unique home button

User Help :

Youtubes help system is easy to use and easy to understand. It presents users with
frequently asked questions and allows them to search a question. This will provide
the user with videos and documents related to their search query.

One big feature missing from Youtubes help system is the ability to sumbit a ticket.
Most services allow a user to send a ticket containing information on their problem to
be reviewed. This is a feature missing from Youtube
